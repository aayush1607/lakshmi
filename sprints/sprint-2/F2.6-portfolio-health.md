# F2.6 — `/portfolio health` Checkup

## One-liner
A 10-second grounded checkup of the user's portfolio: concentration, sector tilt, quality.

## User story
> As a user who doesn't look at their portfolio often, I want Lakshmi to tell me in ten seconds whether something is obviously wrong with how it's built.

## Expected behaviour (PM view)
- User runs `/portfolio health`.
- Verdict is one of: 🟢 Healthy / 🟡 Attention / 🔴 Risky — one line summarising the biggest issue.
- Three sections follow:
  - **Concentration** — top 5 holdings, % weight, flag if any > 10% (or user's own target).
  - **Sector tilt** — a horizontal bar chart of sector weights vs a default diversified benchmark.
  - **Quality** — average quality score (from F2.4) across holdings, flagging the weakest 2–3 names.
- Each flag is an actionable sentence, e.g.:
  > ⚠️ TCS is 14% of portfolio — above the 10% rule of thumb.
- Sources: Zerodha holdings (Tier 2) + Screener fundamentals (Tier 2) + NSE sector map (Tier 1).
- Works offline from cache.

## Acceptance criteria
1. Verdict reflects the worst sub-section (health can't be 🟢 if concentration is 🔴).
2. All percentages sum correctly (± 0.1% rounding).
3. For a new user with 3 holdings, the command still works and the concentration warning makes sense.
4. Runs in under 1 second once holdings and fundamentals are cached.
5. Each flag includes a "Next" suggestion (`/portfolio rebalance`, `/fa <weak-name>`).

## Out of scope
- Rebalance actions (F2.7).
- Tax / risk lenses (F3.6).
- Benchmarking vs user-set custom benchmark.

## Dependencies
- F1.3 (Portfolio), F2.4 (FA data), F1.6 (Response shaper).
