# F3.5 — Yojanas (Long-Horizon Portfolios)

## One-liner
Pre-built, customisable portfolio blueprints for long-term wealth building — coffee-can, all-weather, and goal-based.

## User story
> As a long-term investor, I want Lakshmi to help me **design** a portfolio from scratch — not just analyse what I already have — using proven frameworks.

## Expected behaviour (PM view)
Three yojanas in v1:

### `/yojana coffee-can`
- Lakshmi screens for stocks meeting coffee-can criteria (high ROCE, low debt, 10y+ track record, revenue CAGR > X).
- Proposes a 10–15 stock portfolio with equal weights.
- Shows a backtest of that exact basket over the available history.

### `/yojana all-weather`
- Builds a Ray-Dalio-style diversified allocation adapted for India (equity / debt / gold / cash weights).
- User can specify their risk tolerance to tilt the weights.

### `/yojana goal --amount 2Cr --years 10`
- User states a target corpus and horizon (and optionally monthly SIP capacity).
- Lakshmi reverse-engineers the required CAGR and proposes an allocation likely to meet it — with Monte Carlo bands showing P5/P50/P95 outcomes.

**All yojanas output:**
1. **Allocation** — exact tickers + weights.
2. **Rationale** — why each pick, cited to sources.
3. **Rebalance rule** — e.g. "annually, or on >5% drift".
4. **Expected behaviour** — CAGR range, historical max drawdown, recovery time — from backtest.
5. **Save** — stored as a Mantra so the user can track drift with `/mantra run <name>`.

## Acceptance criteria
1. Every proposed pick has a cited rationale (no unexplained tickers).
2. Backtest period is explicitly shown; no cherry-picked windows.
3. Goal-based yojana correctly computes the required CAGR math and flags unrealistic goals (e.g. "needs 28% CAGR — historically very unlikely").
4. Saved yojanas can be loaded, viewed, and re-backtested.
5. Running `/yojana stress --scenario "2008 repeat"` on an existing yojana replays 2008-09 returns on its composition.

## Out of scope
- Importing smallcases (moved to v1.5).
- Tax-optimised asset location (debt in NPS vs taxable etc.).
- Automatic rebalancing execution.
- International equity / US ADRs.

## Dependencies
- F3.1 (Mantra engine) — yojanas save as mantras.
- F2.1 + F2.4 — need history and fundamentals.

Recording: `/yojana goal --amount 1Cr --years 7 --sip 50000`, walk through the allocation, show Monte Carlo bands, save, re-open, stress test.
